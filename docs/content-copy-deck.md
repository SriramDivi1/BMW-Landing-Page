# BMW M Landing Page — Content & Copy Deck

## Tone & Style Guide

### Voice Principles

| Principle | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Restrained confidence** | Let the machines speak. Copy supports, never shouts. | "600 horsepower" not "AN INCREDIBLE 600 HORSEPOWER!!!" |
| **Museum curator** | Informative, respectful, slightly reverent | "Observe the carbon fiber detailing" not "Check out this sick carbon" |
| **Precise language** | Technical accuracy, no vague superlatives | "Twin-turbocharged inline-six" not "super powerful engine" |
| **White space as content** | Silence between words matters | Short sentences. Room to breathe. |

### Vocabulary

**Use:**
- Craft, engineered, precision, lineage, philosophy
- Observe, experience, explore, discover
- Silhouette, stance, presence, proportion
- Performance, dynamics, capability

**Avoid:**
- Amazing, incredible, awesome, insane
- Best-in-class, world-class, unparalleled
- Luxury (overused), premium (unless necessary)
- Exclamation points (almost never)

### Typography Intent

- **Headlines:** Sparse. 2-5 words maximum. Weight carries meaning.
- **Placards:** 15-25 words. One idea per card.
- **Body:** 40-60 words maximum per section. Scannable.
- **Captions:** 5-10 words. Functional, not decorative.

### Spacing Philosophy

- Generous margins signal premium
- Text floats in space, never crowded
- 3D model is the hero — copy defers
- Vertical rhythm: consistent, predictable, calming

### Exhibit 1: BMW M2

**Headline:**
> Pure.

**Placard Copy:**
> The compact coupe that started a new chapter. Rear-wheel drive. A six-speed manual option. 453 horsepower from a twin-turbocharged inline-six. The M2 strips away everything unnecessary and keeps only what matters: the connection between driver and road.

**Quick Facts (for display):**
- 453 hp / 406 lb-ft
- 0-60: 4.1 seconds
- Rear-wheel drive
- Manual or automatic

**Curator Note (optional hover/expand):**
> The M2 represents BMW M's return to its roots — a driver's car in its most essential form. Every surface, every control, every response calibrated for engagement.

---

### Exhibit 2: BMW M4

**Headline:**
> Statement.

**Placard Copy:**
> Presence before it moves. The M4's widebody stance announces intent from every angle. 503 horsepower. Available all-wheel drive. A grand touring coupe engineered to dominate both track days and coastal highways with equal composure.

**Quick Facts:**
- 503 hp / 479 lb-ft
- 0-60: 3.8 seconds
- RWD or xDrive AWD
- Carbon fiber roof standard

**Curator Note:**
> The M4 Competition's aggressive kidney grille sparked debate — and that's precisely the point. M has never sought consensus. It seeks reaction.

---

### Exhibit 3: BMW M5

**Headline:**
> Duality.

**Placard Copy:**
> The executive athlete. 600 horsepower beneath a sedan's silhouette. The M5 maintains the poise expected in a boardroom while harboring the capability to embarrass sports cars. All-wheel drive with a rear-bias character. Comfort and fury, unified.

**Quick Facts:**
- 600 hp / 553 lb-ft
- 0-60: 3.4 seconds
- M xDrive (AWD)
- Four doors, five seats

**Curator Note:**
> Since 1984, the M5 has defined the super sedan category it created. Four generations later, the formula remains: invisible performance, available on demand.

---

### Exhibit 4: BMW M8

**Headline:**
> Pinnacle.

**Placard Copy:**
> The flagship. 617 horsepower channeled through BMW's most sophisticated chassis. The M8 exists where grand touring meets motorsport — a continent-crossing machine that loses nothing on a race circuit. Carbon ceramic brakes. Adaptive suspension. The culmination of M.

**Quick Facts:**
- 617 hp / 553 lb-ft
- 0-60: 3.0 seconds
- M xDrive (AWD)
- Available as Coupe, Convertible, Gran Coupe

**Curator Note:**
> The M8's twin-turbocharged V8 descends directly from the engine that powered BMW's return to Le Mans. Racing heritage, refined for the road.

### Craft & Engineering Section

**Headline:**
> Fifty years of M.

**Body Copy:**
> In 1972, BMW Motorsport GmbH was founded with a single purpose: to win races. What began on the track evolved into a philosophy — one that infuses every M vehicle with racing DNA.

> The M division doesn't build cars; it builds instruments. Every component is questioned. Every gram is justified. Carbon fiber reduces weight where it matters. Adaptive systems respond in milliseconds. Cooling systems derived from endurance racing ensure performance without compromise.

> This is the craft of M: engineering without apology.
